## Core Principles

These principles guide the design of all assessments and activities:

1. **Practical over theoretical** — Every task must connect to real business/personal use
2. **Iteration is essential** — Reward refinement, not just completion
3. **Personalization matters** — Use OCEAN insights to tailor the experience
4. **AI as partner, not oracle** — Students must *direct* AI, not just receive answers
5. **Data-driven reflection** — Every output should prompt "what does this tell me?"

# WEEK 3: Prompt Engineering Mastery

## Theme: Learning to Speak AI's Language

**Tagline**: *"The quality of your AI output is directly proportional to the quality of your input. Master the prompt, master the result."*

---

### Learning Outcomes
By the end of Week 3, learners will be able to:
1. Apply the RCTFC Framework to any prompt
2. Choose the right AI for different task types
3. Iterate and refine prompts effectively
4. Start a personal prompt library
5. Connect prompt style to OCEAN personality

---

### Core Concepts

#### 1. The RCTFC Framework
Every effective prompt has 5 components:

| Component | Definition | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **R**ole | Persona or expertise to adopt | "You are a conversion copywriter" |
| **C**ontext | Background information | "I run a small bakery targeting busy professionals" |
| **T**ask | Specific action to perform | "Write a promotional email for our new lunch delivery service" |
| **F**ormat | How output should be structured | "Use a friendly tone, keep it under 150 words, include a CTA" |
| **C**onstraints | Limitations to apply | "Don't mention competitors or use the word 'delicious'" |

#### 2. Iteration is Key
- Great prompts rarely happen on the first try
- Refine, adjust, and build on AI responses

#### 3. Context is Queen
- More relevant context = better results
- Your OCEAN profile is context
- Your business goals are context
- Your constraints are context

#### 4. Different AIs, Different Strengths

| AI Tool | Best For |
|---------|----------|
| **ChatGPT** | Creative writing, brainstorming, general tasks |
| **Claude** | Analysis, nuanced reasoning, longer context |
| **Perplexity** | Research, current events, cited sources |

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Activity 1: RCTFC Framework Demo (~20 min)
- Watch instructor demonstrate transforming a vague prompt into a powerful one
- **Example Transformation**:
  - Before: "Write me a marketing email"
  - After: "You are a conversion copywriter (Role). I run a small bakery targeting busy professionals (Context). Write a promotional email for our new lunch delivery service (Task). Use a friendly tone, keep it under 150 words, include a call-to-action button text (Format). Don't mention competitors or use the word 'delicious' (Constraints)."

#### Activity 2: Prompt Engineering Lab (~30 min)
- Using your problem from Senseii Games (Week 2), craft a RCTFC prompt
- Test it on all three platforms:
  - [ChatGPT](https://chat.openai.com)
  - [Claude](https://claude.ai)
  - [Perplexity](https://perplexity.ai)

#### Activity 3: Compare & Refine (~25 min)
- Analyze the three responses
- Which AI gave the best answer for your specific need?
- Refine your prompt and run it again

**Pro Tip**: Your OCEAN profile affects how you communicate:
- High Openness? You might prefer creative prompts
- High Conscientiousness? You'll want structured outputs

#### Activity 4: Build Your Prompt Library (~15 min)
Start a personal "Prompt Library" document with template starters:

```
Business: "You are a [role] helping a [business type]..."
Personal: "Act as my personal [coach/advisor/assistant]..."
Research: "Find and summarize [topic] with sources..."
```

---

### Learning Outcomes

1. Identify a specific business problem worth solving
2. Apply the RCTFC Framework to business scenarios
3. Use OpenCode to generate a simple business solution
4. Evaluate AI outputs for business applicability

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Activity 1: Business Problem Identification Workshop (~15 min)
- Identify 3 pain points in your business or workplace
- Select one problem that is:
  - Specific enough to solve
  - Important enough to matter
  - Within your control to address
- Write a clear problem statement

#### Activity 2: RCTFC Prompt Crafting for Business (~25 min)
- Transform your problem statement into an RCTFC prompt
- Example structure:
  - **Role**: "You are a business operations consultant..."
  - **Context**: "I run a [business type] with [specific challenge]..."
  - **Task**: "Recommend 3 actionable solutions..."
  - **Format**: "Present as a numbered list with pros/cons..."
  - **Constraints**: "Solutions must cost under $500 and take less than 2 weeks to implement..."

#### Activity 3: OpenCode Demo – Build a Simple Tool (~30 min)
- Introduction to OpenCode as a coding assistant
- Watch instructor demo: Creating a simple business tool (e.g., customer follow-up tracker, inventory calculator)
- Key concepts:
  - Describe what you want in plain language
  - Iterate on the output
  - Test and refine

#### Activity 4: Peer Review & Refinement (~20 min)
- Share your RCTFC prompt with a partner
- Provide feedback on clarity and completeness
- Refine your prompt based on feedback

1. Prepare Excel data for AI analysis
2. Use AI to identify trends and patterns in existing business data
3. Generate predictive forecasts for marketing decisions
4. Translate data insights into actionable marketing strategies

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Activity 1: Data Audit – What Data Do You Have? (~15 min)
- Inventory your existing business data:
  - Sales records
  - Customer lists
  - Marketing campaign results
  - Website/social media analytics
- Identify which data is most relevant for forecasting

#### Activity 2: Export & Prep – Formatting Excel for AI (~20 min)
- Export data from your systems (CRM, POS, spreadsheets)
- Clean and format for AI analysis:
  - Remove duplicates and errors
  - Ensure consistent date formats
  - Label columns clearly
  - Create a summary sheet with key metrics
- Best practices for data structure

#### Activity 3: AI Forecasting Lab – Upload, Analyze, Predict (~35 min)
- Upload your Excel file to ChatGPT or Claude
- Prompt structure for forecasting:
  - **Role**: "You are a marketing analytics expert..."
  - **Context**: "This is [business type] data from [time period]..."
  - **Task**: "Analyze trends and forecast [metric] for the next 3 months..."
  - **Format**: "Provide a summary of trends, a 3-month forecast, and confidence level..."
  - **Constraints**: "Focus on [specific product/service/segment]..."
- Document the AI's analysis

#### Activity 4: Strategy Translation – From Forecast to Action (~20 min)
- Review AI-generated insights
- Identify 3 actionable marketing decisions based on the forecast:
  - What to increase
  - What to decrease
  - What to test
- Share one insight with the class

# WEEK 6: Automation & Workflows

## Theme: Set It and Forget It

**Tagline**: *"Set It and Forget It: Building AI-Powered Automation"*

### Strategic Takeaway
> *"The best time you spend is the time you never have to spend again—automate what you can."*

---

### Learning Outcomes
By the end of Week 6, learners will be able to:
1. Identify repetitive tasks suitable for automation
2. Design a simple automation workflow
3. Use OpenCode to generate automation scripts
4. Test and troubleshoot automated workflows

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Activity 1: Automation Opportunity Audit (~15 min)
- List 10 tasks you do repeatedly in your business/work
- Categorize each task:
  - Daily, weekly, or monthly
  - High, medium, or low complexity
  - High, medium, or low value
- Identify top 3 automation candidates

#### Activity 2: Workflow Mapping Exercise (~20 min)
- For your top automation candidate, map the workflow:
  - **Trigger**: What starts the process?
  - **Steps**: What actions happen in sequence?
  - **Output**: What is the end result?
- Draw a simple flowchart (paper or digital)
- Identify decision points and exceptions

#### Activity 3: Build Your First Automation with OpenCode (~35 min)
- OpenCode demo: Generating an automation script
- Prompt structure for automation:
  - **Role**: "You are an automation engineer..."
  - **Context**: "I need to automate [task] that happens when [trigger]..."
  - **Task**: "Write a script/approach that..."
  - **Format**: "Provide step-by-step instructions with code if applicable..."
  - **Constraints**: "Must work with [specific tools/platforms]..."
- Test the generated solution
- Iterate and refine

#### Activity 4: Test & Troubleshoot (~20 min)
- Run your automation
- Identify what works and what doesn't
- Debug common issues:
  - Trigger not firing
  - Data not passing correctly
  - Output format issues
- Document your solution
