## Course Goal

**Primary Goal**: Transform small business owners and professionals into "AI Whisperers" who leverage AI as a strategic partner for data-driven decision-making.

---

## Core Principles

These principles guide the design of all assessments and activities:

1. **Practical over theoretical** — Every task must connect to real business/personal use
2. **Iteration is essential** — Reward refinement, not just completion
3. **Personalization matters** — Use OCEAN insights to tailor the experience
4. **AI as partner, not oracle** — Students must *direct* AI, not just receive answers
5. **Data-driven reflection** — Every output should prompt "what does this tell me?"

# WEEK 3: Financial Gap Analysis & Business Planning

## Theme: From Vision to Reality

**Tagline**: *"A gap analysis without financial data is just a wish list. Connect your vision to your numbers."*

### Strategic Takeaway

1. Upload and analyze financial statements (Income Statement, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow)
2. Connect financial data to Week 2 Vision Board
3. Identify specific gaps between current state and goals
4. Create a Gap-to-Goal Action Plan
5. Use autoworkz.org for financial analysis

---

### Core Concepts

#### 1. The Three Key Financial Statements

| Statement | What It Shows | Key Metrics |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| **Income Statement (P&L)** | Revenue, expenses, profit/loss over time | Revenue, COGS, Gross Profit, Net Profit |
| **Balance Sheet** | Assets, liabilities, equity at a point in time | Assets, Liabilities, Owner's Equity |
| **Cash Flow Statement** | Money moving in and out over time | Operating, Investing, Financing activities |

#### 2. Your 4 Data Points
- **OCEAN Profile** (Week 1) - Who you are naturally
- **Vision Board** (Week 2) - Where you want to go
- **Tech Readiness** (Week 2) - How ready you are for change
- **Financial Reality** (Week 3) - Where you stand today

#### 3. Types of Gaps to Analyze

| Gap Type | Description | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| **Revenue Gap** | Current revenue vs. target | $100K now vs. $250K goal = $150K gap |
| **Customer Gap** | Current customers vs. ideal profile | Local customers vs. regional expansion |
| **Resource Gap** | Available vs. needed resources | 2 employees vs. 5 needed |
| **Time Gap** | Current vs. desired timeline | 3 years to reach goal vs. 1 year desired |

#### 4. Gap-to-Goal Action Plan Structure

| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Current State** | Where you are now (from financials) |
| **Goal State** | Where you want to be (from vision) |
| **Gap** | The difference (measured in $, time, or resources) |
| **Actions** | 3-5 specific steps to close each gap |
| **Timeline** | When will each action be completed? |

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Step 1: Prepare Your Financial Documents (~15 min)
- Gather your three key financial statements
- Ensure documents are current (within last 12 months)
- Have digital copies ready for upload

#### Step 2: Upload Financials to autoworkz.org (~25 min)
- Log into autoworkz.org
- Upload your financial documents
- Review the AI-generated analysis
- Note key metrics and insights

#### Step 3: Map Financial Reality to Your Vision (~20 min)
- Compare your financial analysis with your Week 2 Vision Board
- Identify specific gaps using the Gap Analysis Questions:
  - Revenue Gap: Current revenue vs. your target?
  - Customer Gap: Current customers vs. ideal customer profile?
  - Resource Gap: Do you have the funds to hire/expand?
  - Time Gap: How long to reach your financial goals?

#### Step 4: Create Your Gap-to-Goal Action Plan (~30 min)
- Select top 3 gaps to prioritize
- For each gap, define:
  - Current State (from financials)
  - Goal State (from vision)
  - Gap measurement ($, time, or resources)
  - 3-5 action steps
  - Timeline for completion

# WEEK 4: Marketing & Sales Planning

## Theme: From Gap Analysis to Action Plan

**Tagline**: *"Marketing and sales succeed when you know your customer, price for value, and execute with clear timelines."*

### Strategic Takeaway
> *"Marketing and sales succeed when you know your customer, price for value, and execute with clear timelines."*

---

### Learning Outcomes
By the end of Week 4, learners will be able to:
1. Build a marketing & sales plan based on gap analysis
2. Define a target customer profile with demographics and behaviors
3. Set a pricing strategy aligned with financial goals
4. Create a 90-day action plan with clear milestones

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Step 1: Review Your Week 3 Gap Analysis (~15 min)
- Open your Week 3 Gap-to-Goal Action Plan
- Identify the key gaps:
  - Revenue gap – How much more do you need to earn?
  - Customer gap – How many more customers do you need?
  - Product/service gap – What offerings need improvement?
  - Geography gap – Where are your ideal customers?
  - Team gap – Who do you need to help you grow?
- Key Question: "Which gap, if closed, would have the biggest impact in the next 90 days?"

#### Step 2: Create Your Marketing & Sales Plan with AI (~25 min)
- Use AI with RCTFC prompts to build your marketing strategy
- Example RCTFC Prompt:
  - **Role**: "You are a marketing strategist for small businesses..."
  - **Context**: "My business is [type]. I need to close a $[X] revenue gap by acquiring [Y] new customers..."
  - **Task**: "Create a 90-day marketing plan with 3-5 specific channels..."
  - **Format**: "Present as a table with channel, budget, timeline, and expected results..."
  - **Constraints**: "Budget under $[X], must reach [target audience]..."

#### Step 3: Define Your Target Customer Profile (~20 min)
- Create a detailed profile of your ideal customer:
  - Demographics: Age, income, location, industry
  - Behaviors: Where they spend time, how they buy
  - Pain Points: What problems do they have?
  - Values: What matters most to them?
  - Buying Triggers: What motivates them to purchase?
- Key Questions:
  - Who has the problem my business solves?
  - Where do they spend time online and offline?
  - What motivates them to buy?

#### Step 4: Set Your Pricing Strategy (~20 min)
- Use your financial data from Week 3 to set strategic prices:
  - Cost to deliver (from Week 3 financials)
  - Competitor pricing
  - Value delivered to customer
  - Revenue gap to close
  - Market positioning (premium/competitive/budget)

#### Step 5: Create Your 90-Day Action Plan (~20 min)
- Build a specific, time-bound action plan with milestones:
  - Week 1-2: Foundation activities
  - Week 3-4: Launch first campaign
  - Month 2: Scale what works
  - Month 3: Evaluate and adjust
- Success Metrics: Define how you will measure success (leads, conversions, revenue, customer acquisition cost)

---

### Homework Assignment

#### Task: Marketing & Sales Action Plan (100 points)
1. **Review** your Week 3 Gap Analysis (revenue, customer, product, geography gaps)
2. **Define** your Target Customer Profile (demographics, behaviors, pain points, values, buying triggers)
3. **Develop** your Marketing Strategy (3-5 channels, key messages, budget, metrics)
4. **Set** your Pricing Strategy (costs, competitor pricing, value, final price)
5. **Create** your 90-Day Action Plan (timeline with specific actions and success metrics)

**Submission Requirements**: Word doc, Google Doc, or PDF (2 pages max)

# WEEK 5: AI for Marketing Forecasting

## Theme: Your Data Has a Story

**Tagline**: *"Your Data Has a Story: Using AI to Predict What's Next"*

### Strategic Takeaway
> *"The best predictor of future success is understanding your past patterns—AI helps you see what you've been missing."*

---

### Learning Outcomes
By the end of Week 5, learners will be able to:
1. Prepare Excel data for AI analysis
2. Use AI to identify trends and patterns in existing business data
3. Generate predictive forecasts for marketing decisions
4. Translate data insights into actionable marketing strategies

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Activity 1: Data Audit – What Data Do You Have? (~15 min)
- Inventory your existing business data:
  - Sales records
  - Customer lists
  - Marketing campaign results
  - Website/social media analytics
- Identify which data is most relevant for forecasting

#### Activity 2: Export & Prep – Formatting Excel for AI (~20 min)
- Export data from your systems (CRM, POS, spreadsheets)
- Clean and format for AI analysis:
  - Remove duplicates and errors
  - Ensure consistent date formats
  - Label columns clearly
  - Create a summary sheet with key metrics
- Best practices for data structure

#### Activity 3: AI Forecasting Lab – Upload, Analyze, Predict (~35 min)
- Upload your Excel file to ChatGPT or Claude
- Prompt structure for forecasting:
  - **Role**: "You are a marketing analytics expert..."
  - **Context**: "This is [business type] data from [time period]..."
  - **Task**: "Analyze trends and forecast [metric] for the next 3 months..."
  - **Format**: "Provide a summary of trends, a 3-month forecast, and confidence level..."
  - **Constraints**: "Focus on [specific product/service/segment]..."
- Document the AI's analysis

#### Activity 4: Strategy Translation – From Forecast to Action (~20 min)
- Review AI-generated insights
- Identify 3 actionable marketing decisions based on the forecast:
  - What to increase
  - What to decrease
  - What to test
- Share one insight with the class

# WEEK 6: Automation & Workflows

## Theme: Set It and Forget It

**Tagline**: *"Set It and Forget It: Building AI-Powered Automation"*

### Strategic Takeaway
> *"The best time you spend is the time you never have to spend again—automate what you can."*

---

### Learning Outcomes
By the end of Week 6, learners will be able to:
1. Identify repetitive tasks suitable for automation
2. Design a simple automation workflow
3. Use OpenCode to generate automation scripts
4. Test and troubleshoot automated workflows

---

### In-Class Activities

#### Activity 1: Automation Opportunity Audit (~15 min)
- List 10 tasks you do repeatedly in your business/work
- Categorize each task:
  - Daily, weekly, or monthly
  - High, medium, or low complexity
  - High, medium, or low value
- Identify top 3 automation candidates

#### Activity 2: Workflow Mapping Exercise (~20 min)
- For your top automation candidate, map the workflow:
  - **Trigger**: What starts the process?
  - **Steps**: What actions happen in sequence?
  - **Output**: What is the end result?
- Draw a simple flowchart (paper or digital)
- Identify decision points and exceptions

#### Activity 3: Build Your First Automation with OpenCode (~35 min)
- OpenCode demo: Generating an automation script
- Prompt structure for automation:
  - **Role**: "You are an automation engineer..."
  - **Context**: "I need to automate [task] that happens when [trigger]..."
  - **Task**: "Write a script/approach that..."
  - **Format**: "Provide step-by-step instructions with code if applicable..."
  - **Constraints**: "Must work with [specific tools/platforms]..."
- Test the generated solution
- Iterate and refine

#### Activity 4: Test & Troubleshoot (~20 min)
- Run your automation
- Identify what works and what doesn't
- Debug common issues:
  - Trigger not firing
  - Data not passing correctly
  - Output format issues
- Document your solution

---

### Homework Assignment

#### Task: Automation Implementation (100 points)
1. **Identify**: One repetitive task to automate
2. **Design**: Map the workflow (trigger → steps → output)
3. **Build**: Use OpenCode to create the automation
4. **Test**: Run the automation and document results
5. **Reflect**: What worked? What would you improve?

**Submission Requirements**: Word doc, Google Doc, or PDF with workflow diagram and screenshots (1-2 pages max)
