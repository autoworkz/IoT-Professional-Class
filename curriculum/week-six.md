## Lesson Content

### What is Automation?
Automation uses technology to perform tasks with minimal human intervention.

### Good Automation Candidates
| Criteria | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Repetitive | Done frequently, same steps each time |
| Rule-based | Follows clear, consistent logic |
| Time-consuming | Takes significant time to do manually |
| Error-prone | prone to human error when done manually |

### Tasks NOT to Automate
- Creative work requiring judgment
- Complex decisions with many variables
- Interpersonal communications requiring empathy
- One-time or highly variable tasks

### The Automation Workflow
Every automation has three parts:
1. **Trigger**: What starts the process?
2. **Steps**: What actions happen in sequence?
3. **Output**: What is the end result?

## In-Class Activities

### Activity 1: Automation Opportunity Audit (15 minutes)
**Objective**: Identify automation candidates

**Steps**:
1. List 10 tasks you do repeatedly in your business/work
2. Categorize each task:
   - Daily, weekly, or monthly frequency
   - High, medium, or low complexity
   - High, medium, or low value
3. Identify top 3 automation candidates

---

### Activity 2: Workflow Mapping Exercise (20 minutes)
**Objective**: Design your automation

**Steps**:
1. Choose your top automation candidate
2. Map the workflow:
   - **Trigger**: What starts the process?
   - **Steps**: What actions happen in sequence?
   - **Output**: What is the end result?
3. Draw a simple flowchart
4. Identify decision points and exceptions

---

### Activity 3: Build Your First Automation with OpenCode (35 minutes)
**Objective**: Create automation using AI

**Prompt Structure**:
- **Role**: "You are an automation engineer..."
- **Context**: "I need to automate [task] that happens when [trigger]..."
- **Task**: "Write a script/approach that..."
- **Format**: "Provide step-by-step instructions with code if applicable..."
- **Constraints**: "Must work with [specific tools/platforms]..."

**Steps**:
1. Describe your workflow to OpenCode
2. Review the generated solution
3. Test and iterate
4. Refine based on results

---

### Activity 4: Test & Troubleshoot (20 minutes)
**Objective**: Ensure your automation works

**Steps**:
1. Run your automation
2. Identify what works and what doesn't
3. Debug common issues:
   - Trigger not firing
   - Data not passing correctly
   - Output format issues
4. Document your solution
