## Lesson Content

### The Problem-First Approach
Before using any AI tool, define your problem clearly:
- What exactly are you trying to solve?
- Why does this problem matter?
- What would success look like?

### Writing a Good Problem Statement
A good problem statement is:
- **Specific**: Clearly defined scope
- **Important**: Worth solving now
- **Controllable**: Within your power to address

**Example**:
❌ "I want to grow my business"
✅ "I want to increase repeat customer rate from 20% to 35% within 6 months"

### RCTFC for Business
Business prompts should focus on:
- **ROI** (Return on Investment)
- **Efficiency** improvements
- **Stakeholder** impact
- **Time** and **cost** savings

## In-Class Activities

### Activity 1: Business Problem Identification Workshop (15 minutes)
**Objective**: Define a problem worth solving

**Steps**:
1. Identify 3 pain points in your business or workplace
2. Select one problem that is:
   - Specific enough to solve
   - Important enough to matter
   - Within your control to address
3. Write a clear problem statement

---

### Activity 2: RCTFC Prompt Crafting for Business (25 minutes)
**Objective**: Transform problem into actionable prompt

**Template**:
- **Role**: "You are a business operations consultant..."
- **Context**: "I run a [business type] with [specific challenge]..."
- **Task**: "Recommend 3 actionable solutions..."
- **Format**: "Present as numbered list with pros/cons..."
- **Constraints**: "Solutions must cost under $500 and take less than 2 weeks..."

---

### Activity 3: OpenCode Demo - Build a Simple Tool (30 minutes)
**Objective**: Introduction to OpenCode

**What is OpenCode?**
- AI-powered coding assistant
- No programming required
- Describe what you want in plain language

**Demo Examples**:
- Customer follow-up tracker
- Inventory calculator
- Simple booking system
- Basic data entry form

---

### Activity 4: Peer Review & Refinement (20 minutes)
**Objective**: Improve through feedback

**Steps**:
1. Share your RCTFC prompt with a partner
2. Provide feedback on clarity and completeness
3. Identify what could be improved
4. Refine your prompt based on feedback
