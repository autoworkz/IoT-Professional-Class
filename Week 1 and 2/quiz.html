Quiz: Week 1 — Data, AI & You

Data as the New Gold & AI as the Data Specialist

Strategic Takeaway

"Small businesses that win are not the ones with the most technology, but the ones who ask better questions of their data."

Q1. What is the primary purpose of data in a business context?

Explanation: Data's true value lies in improving decision quality—helping leaders reduce guesswork and align strategy with evidence.

Q2. Which type of data focuses on how people think, act, and interact in a business environment?

Explanation: Behavioral data captures patterns in human behavior, which directly influence leadership, teamwork, and customer engagement.

Q3. Why do many small businesses struggle to make data-driven decisions?

Explanation: Most small businesses rely heavily on experience and instinct, missing opportunities to validate decisions with data.

Q4. What role does Artificial Intelligence (AI) primarily play in business intelligence?

Explanation: AI enhances human decision-making by identifying patterns and insights that may not be immediately visible.

Q5. How can AI and IoT work together in a business setting?

Explanation: IoT gathers real-time data from systems and people, while AI converts that data into actionable intelligence.

Q6. What is the Big 5 (OCEAN) personality assessment primarily used for in business?

Explanation: The Big 5 framework helps organizations understand how personality influences leadership style, communication, and decision-making.

Q7. Which personality trait is most closely associated with organization, reliability, and follow-through?

Explanation: Conscientiousness reflects structure, discipline, and dependability—key traits in execution and operations.

Q8. How can personality data improve business outcomes?

Explanation: When used ethically, personality data helps place people where they perform best and collaborate more effectively.

Q9. Which statement best describes data-driven leadership?

Explanation: Strong leaders balance insight from data with experience—not one or the other.

Q10. What is the biggest business risk of ignoring behavioral and personality data?

Explanation: Ignoring human data leads to misalignment, disengagement, and costly people-related decisions.
